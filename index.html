What is classical conditioning training in cats?

Share on

Intro Text
 
Content

Training cats is a rather laborious task. To achieve this, you first need to understand how your cat learns. Being able to understand and interpret his reaction to new things he encounters will make it much easier for you to change his behavior. 

 

When cats learn something, it's not just about the come, sit, and get up commands that people are training them. However, those who have just adopted a cat think that this is the case. However, cats shape their behavior by learning the events they experience throughout their lives. Therefore, knowing under what conditions and how your cat learns will make it much easier for you to train him. When you decide to train your cats, you need to observe them a lot. In this way, you can train them with a more efficient training. There are also learning types of cats. We explain these learning types for you in the continuation of our article. 

Image

Asocial Learning

Relational learning in cats occurs in two ways. These are Classical Conditioning and Operant Conditioning. The first person to discover the classical method of conditioning was the Russian physiologist Ivan Pavlov. Pavlov, a Nobel laureate, describes classical conditioning this way: "Classical conditioning is a learning process that is carried out in cats using the link between the presence of an environmental stimulus and a naturally occurring stimulus." This method of learning, also known as Pavlovian and Participatory Conditioning, is more attractive to cats and is associated with a biologically used powerful stimulus, such as food, a flatter neutral stimulus that cats are not very interested in.

Another type of learning is Operant Conditioning. The discoverer of Operant Conditioning was Burrhus Frederic (B.F.) Skinner, known as the father of the animal learning genre. What he usually wants to say, and what he talks about a lot in his works, is that classical conditioning is too simple to provide a complete explanation for the complex behavior of animals and will not be effective. That is why the best and most effective way to understand behavior is to believe that an action can be understood by understanding its cause and looking at how its outcome will occur.

 Operant behavior is a type of learning that is said to meet both conditions. Although there is no apparent triggering stimulus, it is freely spread by cats. However, the behavior, which occurs with this training method, is also open to rewards and punishments. Therefore, rewards and/or punishments during training may cause him to be in a better and worse situation.

 How Does Classic Conditioning Work?

 Classical conditioning is a training method in which a neutral signal is reconciled with the instantaneous reflex that occurs naturally in animals. Pavlov, in his previous classical conditioning of dogs, used the neutral signal as a tone of voice and the reflex as a response to food. In doing so, he associated the neutral stimulus with the reflex, allowing the next reaction to take place.
